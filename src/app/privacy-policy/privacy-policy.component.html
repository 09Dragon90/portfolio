Rights under the GDPR

In accordance with Articles 13 and 14 of the GDPR, you have the following rights to ensure fair and transparent data processing:

  • You have the right to access (Article 15 GDPR), meaning you may request confirmation whether we are processing personal data about you and receive a copy of the data.
  • You have the right to rectification (Article 16 GDPR), meaning you may request the correction of incorrect or incomplete data.
  • You have the right to erasure (“right to be forgotten”) (Article 17 GDPR), meaning you may request that we delete your data.
  • You have the right to restriction of processing (Article 18 GDPR), meaning we may only store your data but not use it further.
  • You have the right to data portability (Article 20 GDPR), meaning you may receive your data in a commonly used format and transfer it to another controller.
  • You have the right to object (Article 21 GDPR), meaning you can object to the processing of your data if the processing is based on our legitimate interests.

If you believe that the processing of your data violates data protection law or your data protection rights have been otherwise infringed, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (Article 77 GDPR).
